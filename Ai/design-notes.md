# Atelier Haven Design Notes

## Wireframe / Layout Structure

### Homepage
- Sticky navigation with brand, category links, cart, and checkout
- Hero split layout with oversized editorial headline, primary CTA, secondary CTA, and stacked lifestyle imagery
- Category strip for living, bedroom, dining, and storage
- Featured product grid with large lead card and supporting product cards
- Brand story section with narrative copy, supporting imagery, and service notes
- Service/value section highlighting planning, delivery, and longevity
- Final editorial CTA banner leading into shopping and checkout
- Footer with contact, policies, and social links

### Product Listing
- Intro hero with collection overview
- Left filter rail for price, category, and material
- Right content area with live item count, sorting, and responsive product grid

### Product Detail
- Breadcrumb
- Large primary product gallery with supporting thumbnails
- Sticky purchase panel with product details, materials, dimensions, quantity stepper, and add-to-cart
- Supporting craftsmanship story
- Related products grid

### Cart and Checkout
- Cart review with editable quantities and order summary
- Checkout form with delivery, payment, and review framing
- Minimal order summary sidebar and confirmation state

## UI Design Description

- Palette: warm white, charcoal, layered gray, and soft beige accents
- Typography: `Sora` for display hierarchy and `Manrope` for body copy
- Spacing: generous vertical rhythm, large card radii, wide gutters, and restrained UI chrome
- Imagery: large edge-to-edge product/lifestyle photography with soft overlays and minimal captions
- Motion: smooth scrolling, scroll reveal transitions, image hover scaling, and subtle sticky header behavior
- Tone: premium, quiet, minimal, and editorial without unnecessary decorative clutter

## Sample HTML/CSS

- Core page templates: `index.html`, `products.html`, `product.html`, `cart.html`, `checkout.html`
- Shared premium styling system: `styles.css`
- Shared storefront/cart behavior: `script.js`
